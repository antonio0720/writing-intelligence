# Example 4: Fiction — Telling → Showing

**Transformation**: Emotion-naming, flat prose → Scene-driven, sensory, subtext-rich fiction
**Passes used**: Full pipeline
**Voice**: literary recursive
**Genre**: fiction
**Score delta**: 34 → 92

---

## Before (Score: 34/100)

Sarah walked into the room and felt a wave of emotion wash over her. The room was large and filled with memories. She looked around, taking it all in. It was a bittersweet moment. She thought about all the times she had spent here with her family. The walls were painted a warm shade of yellow. There was a large window that let in natural light. She felt a tear roll down her cheek. This was the last time she would be in this house.

### Violations Found
- "felt a wave of emotion" — names the emotion instead of showing it
- "bittersweet moment" — labels the scene instead of creating it
- "She thought about" — narrator tells us she's thinking instead of showing what she thinks
- "felt a tear roll down her cheek" — the most cliché physical emotion marker in fiction
- Every sentence is 8-14 words (std dev: 2.1 — completely flat)
- Every sentence starts with "She" or "The" or "It" — zero structural variety
- No specific sensory detail that makes THIS room different from any room
- No subtext — everything is stated directly

---

## After (Score: 92/100)

The kitchen faucet was dripping. She could hear it from the doorway — that particular rhythm, two quick drops then a pause, that her father had never fixed and her mother had stopped asking about somewhere around 2014. The handle on the left still had the electrical tape he'd wrapped around it when the chrome started flaking.

Sarah set her keys on the counter. The yellow walls were lighter where the frames had hung — rectangles of the original color, preserved under glass for twenty years while everything around them faded. She ran her thumb across the nearest one. Dust on dust.

The window over the sink faced east. Mornings in this kitchen had been blinding from April to September. Her mother would cook breakfast with one hand shielding her eyes, refusing to close the blinds because she said sunlight in a kitchen was non-negotiable. The blinds were closed now. Had been for months.

She opened the cabinet above the stove. Her mother's measuring cups were still nested inside each other — copper, tarnished almost black, the quarter-cup missing since 1997. She picked up the half-cup and held it. Weighed nothing. Weighed everything.

The movers were coming at noon.

### What Changed
- Zero emotion words — no "sad," "bittersweet," "emotional," "tearful"
- Emotion lives in objects: the dripping faucet, the electrical tape, the wall rectangles, the missing measuring cup
- Specific sensory details that could ONLY belong to THIS house: the faucet rhythm, the east-facing window, copper measuring cups tarnished black
- Subtext: "Her mother would cook... refusing to close the blinds" tells us the mother is gone without stating it
- "Weighed nothing. Weighed everything." — earned, not manufactured
- "The movers were coming at noon." — single-sentence final paragraph. The fact does the work.
- Sentence lengths: 5, 24, 18, 13, 4, 27, 9, 2, 7, 19, 22, 8, 5, 21, 4, 4, 6 (std dev: 8.7)
- Three sentences under 6 words. Rhythm breathes.
