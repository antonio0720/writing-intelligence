# Genre Pack: Government Briefs

## Weighting Adjustments

| Category | Weight Modifier | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Clarity | +5 | Government readers span multiple expertise levels; misinterpretation has policy consequences |
| Specificity | +5 | Budget numbers, statutory references, program names must be exact |
| Evidence Discipline | +5 | Every claim must be traceable to statute, data, or precedent |
| Argument Strength | +3 | Recommendations must survive interagency review |
| Density | +3 | Decision-makers read hundreds of pages daily |
| Memorability | -5 | Function over flair |
| Rhythm | -5 | Clarity over cadence |

## Architecture

### Policy Memo / Decision Brief

1. **Classification / Handling** (if applicable)
2. **Subject line**: Action required + topic + deadline
3. **Issue**: One sentence stating the decision or question
4. **Background**: 2-4 sentences of essential context. Not history — context.
5. **Discussion**: Analysis of options with pros/cons for each
6. **Recommendation**: Specific recommended action with implementing authority
7. **Coordination**: Who has been consulted; who concurs; who non-concurs
8. **Attachments**: Supporting data, statutory references, budget impact

### Legislative Summary / Bill Analysis

1. **Bill identification**: Number, sponsor, committee, status
2. **Purpose**: What the bill does in one sentence
3. **Key provisions**: Organized by title/section, not by importance
4. **Fiscal impact**: CBO score or estimated cost
5. **Stakeholder positions**: Who supports, who opposes, why
6. **Administration position**: If known
7. **Comparison**: How this differs from current law or competing bills

### Agency Report / Congressional Correspondence

1. **Reference**: What prompted this report (FOIA, congressional inquiry, IG referral)
2. **Findings**: Organized by specificity, not by narrative
3. **Statutory authority**: Every action tied to enabling legislation
4. **Recommendations**: Specific, actionable, with responsible office identified
5. **Timeline**: Implementation schedule with milestones

## Voice Notes

- **Plain language mandate**: Federal Plain Writing Act of 2010 applies. Write for a high school reading level unless the audience is exclusively subject-matter experts.
- **Active voice**: "The Secretary shall approve" not "Approval shall be granted by the Secretary."
- **Statutory precision**: Cite U.S.C. sections, not popular names, when referencing law. Include both on first reference: "the Inflation Reduction Act of 2022 (Pub. L. 117-169, 26 U.S.C. § 30D)."
- **Acronyms**: Spell out on first use. Limit to 5-7 per document. If the reader needs a glossary, you have too many.
- **Fiscal references**: Always specify fiscal year. "$4.2B (FY2025)" not "$4.2 billion."
- **Hedging**: Acceptable only when genuinely uncertain. "The Department estimates" is honest. "It could potentially be argued that" is evasion.
- **Neutrality**: Government documents describe options and consequences. They do not advocate unless the document IS the recommendation.

## Formatting Conventions

- Margins: 1 inch all sides
- Font: Times New Roman 12pt or agency equivalent
- Line spacing: Single or 1.15
- Headers: Bold, sentence case
- Page numbers: Bottom center, "Page X of Y"
- Classification markings: Top and bottom of every page (if applicable)
- Paragraphs: Numbered in long documents for reference

## Common Failures

- Burying the recommendation on page 4
- Background section that is a full history instead of essential context
- Options presented without clear pros/cons/risks for each
- Missing fiscal impact
- Missing statutory authority for recommended action
- Acronym soup
- Passive voice hiding who is responsible for what
- Conclusions that don't follow from the analysis
- No coordination block (makes the memo look unofficial)

## Agency-Specific Calibration

Different agencies have different cultures:

- **DOD**: Brevity extreme. Bottom Line Up Front (BLUF). Action-oriented.
- **State Department**: More narrative. Diplomatic hedging acceptable.
- **DOJ**: Legal precision paramount. IRAC structure influences everything.
- **OMB**: Numbers-first. Budget tables before prose.
- **Intelligence community**: Confidence levels stated explicitly. "We assess with moderate confidence..."
- **GAO/IG**: Findings-based. Criteria → Condition → Cause → Effect → Recommendation.

Adjust voice and structure to the target agency.
