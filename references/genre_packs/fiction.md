# Genre Pack: Fiction

## Weighting Adjustments

| Category | Weight Modifier | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Voice Integrity | +5 | Fiction lives or dies on voice |
| Rhythm | +5 | Prose is music in fiction |
| Memorability | +5 | Readers remember lines, images, moments |
| Evidence Discipline | -8 | Replaced by internal consistency and verisimilitude |
| Argument Strength | -5 | Replaced by narrative logic and character motivation |

## Core Principles

### Show, Don't Declare
The character's hand shakes. You don't write "she was nervous."
The rain hits the windshield in sheets. You don't write "the weather was bad."
The reader constructs the emotion from the sensory evidence.

Exception: Interior monologue can declare directly when the POV character
would think in those terms.

### Scene Over Summary
Default to scene. A scene has: a specific time, a specific place, at least
two characters or a character and an environment, and something that changes.
Summary connects scenes. Summary is never the primary mode.

### Dialogue Rules
- Dialogue reveals character through what is said AND what is not said
- No dialogue tag other than "said" unless the manner of speaking is genuinely unclear
- No adverbs on dialogue tags. Ever. "He said angrily" → show the anger in the words or the action.
- People interrupt. People trail off. People lie. People say the opposite of what they mean.
- Each character has a distinct speech pattern. If you cover the names, you should still know who's talking.

### Detail Selection
Not every detail. The RIGHT detail. One precise observation beats five generic ones.
"She wore a red dress" = nothing. "She wore the dress she'd bought for the interview she didn't get" = character, history, emotional state.

### Pacing
- Tension → short sentences, short paragraphs, white space
- Reflection → longer sentences, denser paragraphs
- Climax → compressed. Maximum information per word. Then silence.
- Resolution → breathing room. Longer rhythms. The reader exhales.

### Time
- Linear is the default but not the obligation
- Flashbacks earn their place by revealing information that changes how the reader understands the present
- Flash-forwards create dramatic irony
- Parallel timelines require distinct voice or style markers so the reader always knows when they are

## Common Failures

- Characters who all sound the same in dialogue
- Exposition delivered through dialogue no real person would speak
- Physical descriptions that read like police reports
- Emotional states named instead of demonstrated
- Plots that rely on characters being stupid
- Themes stated explicitly by a character instead of embodied by the narrative
- Weather as mood (unless the weather is doing actual narrative work)
- Dreams as meaningful (they almost never earn their place)
