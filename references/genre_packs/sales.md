# Genre Pack: Sales Copy

## Weighting Adjustments

| Category | Weight Modifier | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Audience Fit | +5 | Wrong audience = zero conversion |
| Specificity | +5 | Vague promises don't convert |
| Density | +3 | Every word costs attention |
| Voice Integrity | +3 | Trust requires consistency |
| Memorability | +3 | The hook must stick |
| Argument Strength | -3 | Replaced by objection anticipation |

## Architecture

### Hook → Story → Offer → Proof → Close

1. **Hook**: One line. Stops the scroll or turns the page. Names the reader's pain or desire with specificity they didn't expect.
2. **Story**: Short narrative that mirrors the reader's experience. They see themselves. Not the writer. Not the product. Themselves.
3. **Offer**: What they get. What it costs. What changes. Concrete. No "transform your life."
4. **Proof**: Testimonials with specifics. Numbers. Before/after with measurable differences. Case studies with named outcomes.
5. **Close**: Clear CTA. One action. No choice paralysis. Risk reversal (guarantee, trial, refund policy).

## Voice Notes

- Write to ONE person, not "you all." Singular second person.
- Name the objection before the reader thinks it. Then resolve it.
- Trust > urgency. False urgency destroys trust. Real urgency states a verifiable reason.
- Price is never hidden. If you're afraid of the price, the offer isn't strong enough.
- Testimonials use the customer's words, not polished rewrites.
- The guarantee is specific: what exactly happens if they're not satisfied?

## Banned Patterns

- "Imagine if..." (earned only after establishing credibility, never as opener)
- "What if I told you..." (never)
- "Limited time offer" without a verifiable deadline
- "Act now" without a reason
- Stacking adjectives: "amazing, incredible, life-changing, revolutionary"
- Fake scarcity: "Only 3 spots left" when there are unlimited spots
- Testimonials without first name, location, or specific outcome
- Before/after without measurable metric

## Common Failures

- Copy that sells features instead of outcomes
- Copy that creates desire but doesn't resolve objections
- CTAs that give too many options
- Proof section weaker than promise section
- Guarantee buried or vague
- Voice that switches from casual in the hook to corporate in the offer
