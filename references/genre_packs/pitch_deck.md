# Genre Pack: Pitch Deck / Investor Presentation

## Weighting Adjustments

| Category | Weight Modifier | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Specificity | +5 | Investors smell vagueness instantly |
| Density | +5 | 10-15 slides. Every word pays rent or gets evicted. |
| Argument Strength | +5 | The narrative must survive due diligence |
| Evidence Discipline | +3 | Claims need numbers, not adjectives |
| Rhythm | -3 | Slide rhythm matters; prose rhythm secondary |
| Memorability | +3 | VCs remember one line per deck. Make it the right one. |

## Slide Architecture

### 1. Title Slide
Company name. One-line description of what you do (not a tagline — a function).
"Stripe: Payments infrastructure for the internet."
NOT: "Reimagining the future of financial technology."

### 2. Problem
Name the problem with specificity. Quantify the pain.
"87% of B2B SaaS companies lose 12-18% of revenue to failed payment recovery.
The average company spends 340 engineering hours/year building retry logic that
still fails 40% of the time."

NOT: "Payments are broken and businesses are struggling."

### 3. Solution
What you built. How it works. One sentence per mechanism.
No screenshots yet — just the logic.

### 4. Why Now
What changed in the market, technology, regulation, or behavior that makes
this possible/necessary NOW and not five years ago?
This slide separates good decks from great ones.

### 5. Market Size
TAM → SAM → SOM with methodology stated.
"TAM: $47B global payment processing (Nilson Report 2024).
SAM: $8.2B B2B SaaS payment recovery (our segment).
SOM: $410M achievable in 3 years at 5% penetration."

NOT: "$50 billion market opportunity."

### 6. Product
Screenshots, demo, or architecture diagram. Show, don't describe.

### 7. Traction
Metrics that matter. Revenue, growth rate, retention, usage.
"$2.4M ARR. 18% MoM growth. 94% net revenue retention. 847 customers."

NOT: "Strong traction with impressive growth."

### 8. Business Model
How you make money. Unit economics. LTV:CAC ratio. Payback period.
Show you understand the math of your own business.

### 9. Competition
Honest competitive landscape. Name competitors directly.
Show your advantage as a structural difference, not a feature checklist.

### 10. Team
Relevant credentials only. Why THIS team for THIS problem.
Not resumes — relevance.

### 11. Ask
Exactly how much. Exactly what it funds. Exactly what milestones it reaches.
"Raising $8M Series A. 18-month runway to $10M ARR, 2,500 customers,
and Series B readiness."

### 12. Closing
One memorable line or data point that crystallizes why this matters.

## Voice Notes

- Active voice only. "We built" not "A solution was developed."
- Numbers replace adjectives. Always.
- Every slide has ONE point. If it has two, split it.
- No "we're the Uber of X" analogies unless the parallel is structural, not aspirational.
- Headings on slides are claims, not labels. "Revenue grew 340% in 12 months" not "Financials."

## Common Failures

- TAM slide without methodology (investors stop reading)
- Competition slide that shows an empty quadrant (investors know it's a lie)
- Team slide that lists impressive titles without relevance to the problem
- Ask slide without milestones (money without a plan)
- "Disruptive" / "Revolutionary" / "Game-changing" anywhere in the deck
- More than 15 slides
- Slides with >50 words of body text
