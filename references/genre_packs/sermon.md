# Genre Pack: Sermons

## Weighting Adjustments

| Category | Weight Modifier | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Rhythm | +5 | The sermon is performed. Cadence IS theology. |
| Voice Integrity | +5 | The congregation must hear their preacher, not a generic voice |
| Memorability | +5 | One line. That's what goes home. Make it the right one. |
| Audience Fit | +3 | The sermon addresses THIS congregation in THIS moment |
| Evidence Discipline | -5 | Replaced by scriptural grounding, testimony, and lived experience |
| Density | -3 | Sermons need breathing room. Repetition builds, it doesn't pad. |

## Architecture

### Opening (The Hook)
2-3 minutes. Establish shared reality.
Options:
- A moment from the week (specific: "I was standing in the checkout line at H-E-B on Tuesday...")
- A question the congregation carries ("How many of you have prayed for something and heard nothing back?")
- A scripture read fresh, as if for the first time

Never: "Today I want to talk about..." (throat-clearing, not preaching)

### Foundation (The Text)
3-5 minutes. Scriptural grounding.
- Read the passage. Let it land.
- Context: Who wrote it. When. To whom. Under what conditions.
- The uncomfortable part: What in this text challenges comfort? Start there.

### Body (The Argument)
8-12 minutes. Three movements maximum.

Each movement:
1. Scriptural observation (what does the text say?)
2. Contemporary grounding (what does that look like in THIS life?)
3. Escalation (what does that demand of us?)

Build intensity across the three movements. The first is observational.
The second is personal. The third is prophetic.

### The Turn
1-2 minutes. The moment the sermon shifts from diagnosis to prescription.
From "this is what's broken" to "this is what God offers."

The turn is the hinge. Everything before it builds pressure.
Everything after it releases purpose.

### Climax (The Declaration)
2-3 minutes. Peak intensity. This is where cadence reaches full power.

- Sentences shorten to percussive declarations
- Anaphora builds: "God didn't bring you this far to..." / "When they said you couldn't..."
- Volume and intensity peak
- The congregation responds (write for the response — leave space)

### Landing (The Close)
1-2 minutes. The voice drops. Intensity resolves to intimacy.

Options:
- A return to the opening image, now transformed
- A single quiet declaration of truth
- A specific invitation (not generic: "If you're carrying the weight of a decision you made in March, and you haven't forgiven yourself for it...")
- A prayer that is specific to this sermon, not a generic benediction

## Voice Notes

See `voiceprints/sermon_black_church.md` for detailed voice parameters.

Key principles:
- **Specificity of testimony**: "I was 23 years old, working third shift at the warehouse on MLK Boulevard" beats "I was going through a hard time."
- **Scriptural integration**: Woven into the prose, not block-quoted. The congregation knows the text.
- **Dignity**: The congregation is never pitied. They are addressed as people with agency and spiritual capacity.
- **Silence**: Pauses are architectural. Write them. A period and a line break IS a pause.
- **Humor**: Early in the sermon. Warm. Knowing. Laughter of recognition. Never mockery.

## Cadence Architecture

The sermon's rhythm follows a musical structure:

```
Opening:     ♩ ♩ ♩ ♩         (even, conversational, steady)
Foundation:  ♩ ♩ ♪ ♩ ♪       (slightly varied, building interest)
Body 1:      ♩ ♪♪ ♩ ♪♪ ♩     (rhythm developing, variety increasing)
Body 2:      ♪♪ ♩ ♪♪♪ ♩ ♪♪   (intensity building, more variation)
Body 3:      ♪♪♪ ♩ ♪♪♪♪ ♩    (near-peak, rapid with anchoring beats)
Climax:      ♬♬♬ ♩! ♬♬♬ ♩!   (peak intensity, short percussive declarations)
Landing:     ♩ . . . ♩ . .    (slow, spacious, silence between notes)
```

Sentence lengths follow this arc:
- Opening: 12-18 words average
- Body: 10-20 words, increasing variance
- Climax: 5-12 words, mostly short declaratives
- Landing: 8-15 words, measured pace

## Common Failures

- Generic comfort without specific engagement ("God has a plan")
- Prosperity gospel framing (faithfulness = material reward)
- All diagnosis, no prescription (the congregation leaves condemned, not empowered)
- All prescription, no diagnosis (the sermon doesn't earn its demand)
- Flat cadence — same intensity from opening to close
- Scripture quoted but never engaged with (decoration, not grounding)
- Illustration that serves the preacher's ego, not the congregation's need
- Closing with "Let us pray" after a generic conclusion
