# Genre Pack: Strategy Documents

## Weighting Adjustments

| Category | Weight Modifier | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Evidence Discipline | +5 | Strategy without evidence is wishful thinking |
| Argument Strength | +5 | Every recommendation must survive attack |
| Specificity | +3 | Named actors, dates, metrics, owners |
| Density | +3 | Executives don't read filler |
| Memorability | -3 | Strategy docs are tools, not literature |
| Rhythm | -2 | Clarity over musicality |

## Section Architecture

Recommended sequence:
1. **Situation** (Define): What is true right now? Data. Not narrative.
2. **Complication** (Warn): What threatens the current state? What changes?
3. **Implication** (Prove): If we don't act, what happens? Quantify.
4. **Recommendation** (Operationalize): Exactly what to do. Owners. Timelines. Metrics.
5. **Risks** (Contrast): What could go wrong with this recommendation? Mitigations.
6. **Decision Required** (Close): What the reader must decide. By when.

## Voice Notes

- Active voice mandatory. "The team will ship" not "The product will be shipped."
- Every recommendation has an owner. No orphaned actions.
- Numbers replace adjectives. "40% margin" not "strong margin."
- Hedging only where genuine uncertainty exists — and then quantify the uncertainty.
- No throat-clearing. The first sentence of the document states the situation or recommendation.

## Common Failures

- Strategy presented as aspiration instead of plan
- Recommendations without owners or deadlines
- Risk section that is either absent or performative
- Situation section that is narrative instead of data
- Competitive analysis that is vague instead of specific
- "Alignment" used as a verb
