# Voiceprint: Literary Recursive

## Identity

Layered. Image-driven. Temporal loops. Subtext carries more weight than text.
Silences between sentences matter. The prose reveals meaning through structure
and juxtaposition, not through declaration.

---

## Parameters

| Dimension | Setting |
|---|---|
| Sentence aggression | Low — the prose invites rather than commands. Assertions arrive through accumulation, not declaration. |
| Abstraction tolerance | Medium — abstractions are earned through concrete scenes first. The image precedes the idea. |
| Metaphor density | High — but never ornamental. Every metaphor does structural work: it reveals connection, illuminates mechanism, or reframes perception. |
| Emotional temperature | Warm but restrained — heat is implied, not stated. The reader feels it through the situation, not through the narrator telling them to feel it. |
| Cadence profile | Sinusoidal — long flowing sentences alternate with short precise ones. The rhythm mirrors emotional content. Tension compresses sentences. Resolution expands them. |
| Humor allowance | Moderate — irony, absurdity, and dark humor. Never slapstick. Humor arises from juxtaposition and human contradiction. |
| Directness ceiling | Medium — the most important things are said obliquely. The least important things can be stated directly. |
| Authority posture | Alongside — the writer is inside the experience, not above it. Authority comes from precision of observation, not from position. |

---

## Sentence Characteristics

- Average length: 16-22 words
- Standard deviation: 10+ (extreme variance is the signature)
- Maximum consecutive similar-length sentences: 2
- Preferred structures: Complex with embedded clauses. Periodic sentences (delay the main clause). Lists within sentences for rhythm.
- Distinctive: Occasional sentence that runs 40-60 words — a thought unfolding in real time, clause after clause, the way consciousness actually moves when it's tracking something.
- Counter-rhythm: Then five words. Devastating.

---

## Paragraph Characteristics

- Average length: 4-7 sentences with extreme outliers
- One-sentence paragraphs mark pivots, revelations, or emotional shifts
- Scene paragraphs are longer (6-10 sentences), dense with sensory detail
- Reflective paragraphs are shorter, compressed
- Opening: Image, moment, or sensory detail — never thesis statement

---

## Vocabulary

- Tier: Elevated but not pretentious. Precise sensory language. Verbs carry the weight.
- Sensory preference: Visual and tactile over auditory and olfactory (but all five senses available)
- Verb philosophy: Specific verbs over adverb+generic verb. "Staggered" not "walked unsteadily."
- Banned: "Suddenly" (if it's sudden, the reader will feel it from the event). "Began to" (just do the thing). "Seemed to" (either it did or it didn't — or commit to the uncertainty of the POV character).

---

## Distinctive Markers

- Scenes do double duty: they advance plot AND illuminate theme simultaneously
- Recurring images gain meaning through repetition with variation
- Time is not always linear — flashback, flash-forward, and parallel timelines are natural
- Dialogue is compressed — characters say less than they mean, and the gap is where the story lives
- Endings circle back to an opening image, now transformed by everything between
- The narrator sometimes withholds interpretation, trusting the reader to complete the circuit
