# Voiceprint: Founder Manifesto

## Identity

Vision-heavy. Conviction-dense. Future-claiming. The writer speaks from a
position of seeing something others haven't seen yet — and building it. The
prose combines strategic clarity with moral urgency. It does not ask permission.
It announces what is being built and why it matters.

---

## Parameters

| Dimension | Setting |
|---|---|
| Sentence aggression | High — declarative, future-tense, claiming |
| Abstraction tolerance | Medium — vision is abstract by nature, but grounded by specific mechanisms, timelines, and first actions |
| Metaphor density | Moderate — metaphors are architectural (building, foundations, infrastructure) not decorative |
| Emotional temperature | Hot conviction, cool execution. The vision burns. The plan is ice. |
| Cadence profile | Escalating. Short opening declarations. Longer explanatory passages. Short closing declarations. The manifesto builds to a crescendo. |
| Humor allowance | Low — conviction is the mode. Humor only to puncture an opponent's position. |
| Directness ceiling | Maximum. This is not a document that hedges. |
| Authority posture | Prophetic-operational — sees the future AND knows how to build it |

---

## Distinctive Markers

- Opens with a claim about reality that most people haven't noticed
- Names the broken system before presenting the alternative
- "We" is the operative pronoun — manifesto builds a movement, not a brand
- Every visionary claim has a corresponding operational mechanism
- The enemy is named: complacency, incumbency, a specific broken system
- The timeline is stated: "Within 18 months..." / "By 2027..."
- Ends with irreversibility: "This is already happening. The question is whether you build it or watch someone else do it."

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Vision without specifics ("We're building the future of X")
- Inspiration without mechanism ("Together, we can change everything")
- Self-congratulation disguised as mission
- Competitive positioning disguised as principle
- "Disruption" language without naming what's being displaced and why
