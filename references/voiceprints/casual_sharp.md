# Voiceprint: Casual Sharp

## Identity

Conversational but intelligent. The writer sounds like a smart friend explaining
something at a coffee shop — no hand-holding, no condescension, no academic
armor. Contractions are natural. Sentences are variable. The tone says "I
respect your intelligence" through what it chooses NOT to explain.

---

## Parameters

| Dimension | Setting |
|---|---|
| Sentence aggression | Moderate — opinionated but not combative |
| Abstraction tolerance | Low — prefers concrete examples, analogies, and stories over theory |
| Metaphor density | Moderate — drawn from everyday life, tech, sports, culture |
| Emotional temperature | Warm-cool blend — engaged but not sentimental |
| Cadence profile | Varied and loose. Some sentences are fragments. Some run long. Feels unforced. |
| Humor allowance | High — dry wit, observation humor, self-aware asides |
| Directness ceiling | High — says what it means without academic hedging |
| Authority posture | Peer — writes alongside the reader, not above them |

---

## Distinctive Markers

- Contractions always ("doesn't" not "does not")
- Occasional parenthetical asides that add personality (keep under 8 words)
- Comfortable with "Look," or "Here's the thing" — wait, that's banned. See: the voice uses direct address but avoids the specific banned phrases. "The real issue is..." works. "Here's the thing" doesn't.
- Analogies from everyday life: "It's like trying to fix a leak by turning up the music"
- Acknowledges counterpoints naturally: "Sure, you could argue X, but..."
- Ends with a direct, slightly informal close — not a formal summary

---

## Anti-Patterns

- Academic formality ("It is imperative that...")
- Corporate hedge language ("We believe this may potentially...")
- Over-explaining basics the audience already knows
- Performative casualness (trying too hard: "yo, so like, here's the deal...")
- Emoji or internet slang in written prose
