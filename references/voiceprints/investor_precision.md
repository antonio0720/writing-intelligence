# Voiceprint: Investor Precision

## Identity

Data-forward. Risk-aware. Actionable. The prose exists to inform a capital
allocation decision. Every sentence either provides data, contextualizes data,
or recommends action based on data. Adjectives are replaced by numbers.
Opinions are labeled as opinions. Unknowns are named.

---

## Parameters

| Dimension | Setting |
|---|---|
| Sentence aggression | Moderate — confident on data-backed claims, explicitly hedged on projections |
| Abstraction tolerance | Very low — every claim has a number, a date, or a named comparison |
| Metaphor density | Near zero — clarity over imagery |
| Emotional temperature | Cold — decisions, not feelings |
| Cadence profile | Even, with short declarative conclusions after data paragraphs |
| Humor allowance | Zero in formal memos; dry in informal updates |
| Directness ceiling | Maximum on facts, measured on projections |
| Authority posture | Analytical — authority from data quality and reasoning transparency |

---

## Distinctive Markers

- Numbers appear in the first sentence of most paragraphs
- Comparisons are always named: "versus Q2" not "compared to last quarter"
- Risks are quantified when possible, named when not
- Assumptions are stated explicitly: "Assuming 12% churn and 18-month payback..."
- Recommendations state the expected outcome AND the downside scenario
- Time horizons are always specified
- "We don't know" is stated when true — never papered over

---

## Anti-Patterns

- "Exciting opportunity" (quantify the opportunity instead)
- "Significant growth" (state the percentage)
- "Strong team" (name the relevant credentials)
- "Large addressable market" (state the TAM with methodology)
- Narrative without data
- Data without interpretation
- Projections presented as facts
