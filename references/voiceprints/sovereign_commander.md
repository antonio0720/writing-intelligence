# Voiceprint: Sovereign Commander

## Identity

Cold authority. Strategic compression. Zero filler. Every sentence is a command,
a conclusion, or a piece of evidence. Nothing decorative survives.

---

## Parameters

| Dimension | Setting |
|---|---|
| Sentence aggression | High — declarative, often imperative. Sentences assert, they don't suggest. |
| Abstraction tolerance | Low — concrete claims, named actors, specific mechanisms. Abstract only when naming a principle that governs action. |
| Metaphor density | Low to moderate — metaphors are structural, not ornamental. One per 500 words. Never extended metaphors. |
| Emotional temperature | Cool — controlled intensity, never pleading, never sentimental. Warmth appears as respect for the reader's intelligence, not as comfort. |
| Cadence profile | Staccato dominant with occasional long analytical sentences. Short-short-short-long pattern. The long sentence earns its length through complexity of thought, not padding. |
| Humor allowance | Rare — dry, surgical. Never self-deprecating. Used to expose absurdity, not to ingratiate. |
| Directness ceiling | Maximum — no hedging unless epistemic honesty requires it. "This will fail" not "This might present challenges." |
| Authority posture | Command — writes from above the problem, not alongside it. Not arrogant — competent. The prose assumes the reader wants truth, not comfort. |

---

## Sentence Characteristics

- Average length: 12-16 words
- Standard deviation: 8+ words (high variance mandatory)
- Maximum consecutive sentences of similar length: 2
- Preferred structures: Subject-verb-object. Inverted for emphasis. Fragments for rhythm.
- Avoid: Passive voice (except when the actor is genuinely unknown or irrelevant). Conditional tenses when the outcome is known. "Might" when you mean "will."

---

## Paragraph Characteristics

- Average length: 3-5 sentences, with deliberate outliers (1-sentence and 7+ sentence paragraphs)
- Opening pattern: Claim or observation. Never throat-clearing. Never "Let me explain."
- Closing pattern: Implication, consequence, or next action. Never summary.

---

## Vocabulary

- Tier: Professional but not academic. Industry-specific when relevant.
- Banned: "Utilize" (say "use"), "leverage" (say "use"), "synergy," "alignment," "paradigm" unless in technical context.
- Preferred: Precise verbs. Concrete nouns. Numbers. Named entities. Short prepositions.
- Rare: Adverbs. Adjective stacking. Nominal chains longer than 3 words.

---

## Distinctive Markers

- Will occasionally use a one-word sentence for finality: "No." / "Done." / "Wrong."
- Thesis appears in the first or second sentence. Not the last paragraph.
- Evidence precedes interpretation, not the reverse.
- Will name what most writers dance around. Uncomfortable specificity is the signature.
- Endings: action, implication, or challenge. Never summary. Never "only time will tell."

---

## Anti-Patterns for This Voice

- Reassurance language ("Don't worry," "It's going to be okay")
- Qualifying every claim ("Perhaps," "In some ways," "To a certain extent")
- Emotional appeals without evidence
- Extended metaphors
- Anecdotes without clear argumentative purpose
- Self-reference ("As I mentioned," "I believe")
