# Style Transfer Engine

## Purpose

Convert prose from any source genre/voice to any target genre/voice while
preserving the core argument, evidence, and meaning. This is not paraphrasing.
It is structural reconstruction — the same load carried by a different vehicle.

---

## Transfer Protocol

### Step 1: Content Extraction

Before transferring, decompose the source text into content atoms:

- **Claims**: What positions does the text assert?
- **Evidence**: What facts, data, examples, quotes support each claim?
- **Logic**: What is the argumentative sequence? (If A, then B, therefore C)
- **Stakes**: What's at risk? What changes if the reader acts or doesn't?
- **Audience assumptions**: What does the source text assume the reader knows?
- **Emotional arc**: What does the text want the reader to feel, and in what order?

Strip all style. What remains is the payload.

### Step 2: Target Analysis

Load the target genre pack and/or voiceprint. Extract:

- Section architecture requirements
- Sentence-level constraints (length, structure, variance targets)
- Vocabulary tier and domain terms
- Evidence presentation conventions
- Emotional temperature range
- Pacing expectations
- Opening and closing conventions

### Step 3: Structural Mapping

Map source content atoms to target architecture:

| Source Element | Target Placement |
|---|---|
| Central claim | Where does the target genre place the thesis? (Opening? After evidence? Implied?) |
| Supporting evidence | How does the target genre integrate evidence? (Inline? Footnoted? Narrated? Data tables?) |
| Counterarguments | Does the target genre engage opposition? (Steelman? Dismiss? Ignore? Absorb?) |
| Emotional content | How does the target genre handle emotion? (Stated? Implied? Embodied in scene? Suppressed?) |
| Call to action | Does the target genre end with action? (Direct? Implied? Challenged? None?) |

### Step 4: Reconstruction

Rebuild the text in the target genre's conventions. This is not line-by-line
rewriting. It is a full architectural reconstruction.

### Step 5: Voice Application

Apply the target voiceprint. Restore rhythm, vocabulary, and personality
markers per the voiceprint spec.

### Step 6: Verification

Check that:
- [ ] All content atoms from the source survive in the target
- [ ] No content was added that wasn't in the source (no hallucination during transfer)
- [ ] The target genre's conventions are satisfied
- [ ] The target voiceprint's parameters are met
- [ ] The output passes the anti-pattern checks

## Transfer Matrix

Common transfers and their key challenges:

### Academic → Strategy
- Strip hedging. Replace "suggests" with "shows" where evidence is strong.
- Convert literature review into situation analysis.
- Replace citations with named data points.
- Add owners, timelines, and decision points.
- Cut methodology unless the reader needs to evaluate it.
- Challenge: Preserving epistemic honesty while removing academic caution.

### Academic → Sermon
- Identify the human story inside the data.
- Ground statistics in congregational reality.
- Replace citations with testimony and scriptural parallels.
- Build cadence architecture: observation → scripture → escalation → declaration.
- Add call-response rhythmic structure.
- Challenge: Maintaining factual accuracy while shifting to oral, emotional register.

### Strategy → Pitch Deck
- Compress situation/complication into 2 slides.
- Convert recommendation into product/solution framing.
- Replace narrative evidence with metrics.
- Add competitive positioning and market sizing.
- End with a specific ask.
- Challenge: Compressing 10 pages into 12 slides without losing the logic.

### Fiction → Cinematic Narration
- Reduce interiority. Characters act; they don't reflect.
- Convert "she thought" passages into visual behavior.
- Apply shot-based structure to every paragraph.
- Cut narrator commentary. Let scenes carry meaning.
- Increase sensory density. Reduce abstraction.
- Challenge: Preserving subtext when interiority is removed.

### Sales → Academic
- Replace emotional appeals with evidence citations.
- Convert testimonials into case study format.
- Replace urgency language with analytical framing.
- Add limitations, qualifications, and alternative explanations.
- Restructure from persuasion arc to argument arc.
- Challenge: Maintaining the core claim's power while adding epistemic rigor.

### Email → Speech
- Expand compressed points into developed paragraphs.
- Add breathing room and landing lines.
- Convert bullet points into narrative sequence.
- Add audience engagement cues (questions, direct address).
- Build toward a climactic moment.
- Challenge: Expanding without padding. Every new sentence must earn its space.

### Investor Memo → Founder Manifesto
- Shift from analytical distance to prophetic conviction.
- Convert market analysis into vision of the future.
- Replace risk section with obstacle-overcoming narrative.
- Shift from third-person analysis to first-person-plural declaration.
- Add moral urgency alongside financial opportunity.
- Challenge: Maintaining credibility while adding conviction.

### Legal → General Public
- Replace legal terminology with plain language equivalents.
- Convert IRAC structure into narrative.
- Replace citations with named examples.
- Reduce sentence complexity. Active voice dominant.
- Add context the public doesn't have.
- Challenge: Maintaining legal precision while becoming accessible.

### Sermon → Essay
- Convert oral cadence to written rhythm.
- Replace scriptural authority with analytical evidence.
- Convert call-response energy into argument escalation.
- Reduce repetition (earned in speech, redundant in print).
- Maintain dignity and moral seriousness.
- Challenge: Preserving the force of conviction without the oral delivery.

### Any → Dialogue
- Identify the core tension or disagreement in the source.
- Assign positions to characters.
- Let characters argue the points the source text analyzes.
- Convert evidence into what characters would actually say.
- Add subtext — characters don't lecture, they negotiate.
- Challenge: Making the argument feel like a real conversation, not a debate transcript.

## Transfer Quality Checks

After every transfer, verify:

1. **Content fidelity**: Did any claims, evidence, or logic get lost?
2. **Genre compliance**: Does the output follow the target genre's conventions?
3. **Voice integrity**: Does the output match the target voiceprint?
4. **Anti-pattern clean**: Does the output pass all anti-pattern checks?
5. **Audience fit**: Is the output calibrated for the target audience?
6. **No smuggled style**: Did source-genre conventions leak into the target?
   (Academic hedging in a manifesto. Sales urgency in a legal memo.)

---

## Chaining Transfers

For maximum undetectability in academic contexts:

1. Write the content in the student's natural voice (casual, thinking-through-it)
2. Transfer to academic register while preserving the original's imperfections
3. Run the academic genre pack
4. Apply the academic voiceprint
5. Run the anti-detection adversarial checks

The content started human. Each transfer preserved the human origin. The
academic polish doesn't erase the authorship — it refines it.
