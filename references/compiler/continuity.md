# Continuity System

## Purpose

For multi-chapter books, multi-section strategy documents, recurring brand
voice, or any project spanning multiple writing sessions — maintain consistency
across the entire body of work.

---

## Continuity Dimensions

### Voice Continuity
Track across the entire project:
- Sentence length mean and standard deviation per chapter/section
- Vocabulary tier (is Chapter 5 suddenly more academic than Chapters 1-4?)
- Metaphor frequency and type consistency
- Emotional temperature range
- Authority posture consistency

Flag: Any section whose voice metrics deviate by >20% from the project average
without narrative justification (e.g., a flashback chapter may intentionally
shift voice).

### Thesis Continuity
For non-fiction:
- The central thesis stated in the introduction must be traceable through every
  subsequent section
- Each section's sub-thesis must connect back to the central thesis
- No section should contradict the central thesis without acknowledging the
  contradiction as intentional tension

Flag: Any section where the central thesis is absent for >1000 words.

### Character Voice (Fiction)
For each named character:
- Speech patterns (sentence length, vocabulary, contractions, formality)
- Verbal tics or signature phrases
- Topics they avoid
- How they address other characters
- Emotional register range

Flag: Any dialogue where a character speaks outside their established patterns
without narrative justification.

### Metaphor Registry
Track every metaphor used:
- What was compared to what
- In which section/chapter
- Whether it recurs

Flag:
- Same metaphor used unintentionally in different sections (feels lazy)
- Contradictory metaphors for the same concept
- Metaphor clusters that overlap (mixing metaphors across paragraphs)

Allow: Intentional recurrence where a metaphor deepens through repetition.

### Terminology Lock
For technical, strategic, or branded writing:
- The same concept must use the same word throughout
- If "Revenue Operating System" is the term, it doesn't become "revenue platform"
  in Section 4 and "sales engine" in Section 7
- Create a glossary at project start. Enforce it.

Flag: Any synonym substitution for a locked term.

### Contradiction Detection
Cross-reference claims across sections:
- Does Section 3 claim X while Section 7 claims not-X?
- Does a character know something in Chapter 4 that they didn't learn until Chapter 6?
- Does a timeline contradict itself?

Flag: Any factual or logical contradiction.

### Emotional Arc Continuity
For narrative or persuasive longform:
- Map the intended emotional trajectory (tension curve)
- Identify the emotional register of each section
- Verify the arc builds, releases, and resolves as designed

Flag: Emotional plateau (3+ sections at the same intensity level). Unearned
climax (peak intensity without sufficient buildup). Missing resolution.

---

## Implementation

At the start of a longform project, establish:

1. **Voice profile**: Baseline metrics for the project's voice
2. **Terminology glossary**: Locked terms and their definitions
3. **Character registry**: (fiction) Each character's voice parameters
4. **Metaphor log**: Running list of metaphors used
5. **Timeline**: (fiction/narrative) Chronological event sequence
6. **Claim register**: (non-fiction) Central thesis + all sub-theses

Before writing each new section:
- Review the registers above
- Verify the new section's voice parameters align with the project baseline
- Check for potential contradictions with existing content
- Confirm the emotional arc position

After completing each section:
- Update all registers
- Run contradiction check against previous sections
- Verify terminology consistency
- Score voice continuity
