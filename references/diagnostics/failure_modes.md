# Failure Modes

## Purpose

A taxonomy of writing failures organized by severity. Each mode includes:
what it looks like, why it happens, how to detect it, and how to fix it.

---

## Critical Failures (Auto-Fail: Score Capped at 65)

### F-01: Fabricated Evidence
The text cites a source, statistic, quote, or study that does not exist.

Detection: Every citation, number, and attribution checked against verifiable sources.
Fix: Remove the claim or find real evidence. Flag as unverified if uncertain.

### F-02: Perplexity Flatness
5+ consecutive sentences where every word is the statistically expected next word. The text reads as if generated by autocomplete.

Detection: Mental model: read each sentence and ask "could I predict almost every word in this sentence given the previous one?" If yes for 5+ in a row, flag.
Fix: inject_variance operator. Replace predictable words with precise, unexpected alternatives. Restructure sentences.

### F-03: Hard-Ban Saturation
3+ hard-ban phrases from phrases.md appear in the text.

Detection: Pattern match against hard-ban list.
Fix: Delete and restructure. Every hard-ban phrase has a better alternative (or no alternative — the sentence didn't need to exist).

### F-04: Argumentative Vacuum
The text contains no actual argument — just a sequence of assertions, descriptions, or restatements.

Detection: Remove every sentence that is not a claim supported by evidence. If nothing remains, the text has no argument.
Fix: Identify the thesis. Build evidence chains for each supporting claim. Structure for escalation.

### F-05: Voice Absence
The text could have been written by any LLM. No distinctive patterns, vocabulary, or rhythmic signature.

Detection: Cover the author attribution. Could this be any of 10 different writers? If yes, voice is absent.
Fix: Apply voiceprint. Inject distinctive markers. Restore rhythm signature.

## Severe Failures (Score Capped at 75)

### F-06: Cadence Lock
The text falls into a single rhythmic pattern for 200+ words. Same sentence lengths, same structures, same transitions.

Detection: Count sentence lengths across any 200-word span. Standard deviation below 4 = cadence lock.
Fix: Deliberate variance injection. Follow a pattern of short-long-medium-very short-very long.

### F-07: Evidence Desert
300+ words without a single named source, number, example, or verifiable claim.

Detection: Scan for specifics. If a passage exceeds 300 words with zero grounding elements, flag it.
Fix: Scene grounding techniques. Add at least one specific per 100 words.

### F-08: Structural Drift
Sections don't perform their declared jobs. A "Prove" section only asserts. A "Define" section rambles.

Detection: Section architecture audit. Declare each section's job, then verify.
Fix: Rewrite the section to perform its job or reassign its job to match its actual content.

### F-09: Audience Mismatch
The text addresses the wrong reader. Technical jargon for a general audience. Oversimplification for experts.

Detection: Identify the intended audience. Then check vocabulary tier, abstraction level, and evidence expectations against that audience.
Fix: audience_shift operator. Adjust register, vocabulary, and depth.

### F-10: Recursive Restatement
The same idea appears 3+ times in different words without new information.

Detection: Summarize each paragraph in one sentence. If two summaries are identical, one paragraph is redundant.
Fix: Keep the strongest version. Delete the others. Use the space for evidence or a new point.

---

## Moderate Failures (Score Penalty: -5 to -10)

### F-11: Soft-Ban Accumulation
More than 5 soft-ban items in 1000 words (excessive adverbs, em dashes, "However" openers, etc.).

### F-12: Transition Repetition
The same transition word or phrase appears 3+ times in 1000 words.

### F-13: Opening Weakness
The first sentence is throat-clearing, context-setting, or definitional instead of claim, scene, or data.

### F-14: Closing Collapse
The final paragraph summarizes instead of extending, challenging, or crystallizing.

### F-15: Paragraph Length Uniformity
All paragraphs within ±1 sentence of each other across 500+ words.

### F-16: Abstraction Float
3+ consecutive sentences at the top of the abstraction ladder without grounding.

### F-17: Unearned Emotion
Emotional language without earned context. Telling the reader to feel something without showing them why.

### F-18: Over-Qualification
Every claim hedged to the point of saying nothing. "It might perhaps be possible that some evidence could potentially suggest..."

### F-19: Dead Metaphor Accumulation
3+ dead metaphors in 500 words ("the bottom line," "a level playing field," "moved the goalposts").

### F-20: Concept Overload
More than 3 novel concepts introduced in a single paragraph without examples or breathing room.

---

## Minor Failures (Score Penalty: -1 to -3)

### F-21: Occasional adverb that adds nothing
### F-22: One instance of passive voice where active would be stronger
### F-23: A transition that could be smoother
### F-24: A paragraph that runs slightly long
### F-25: A sentence that could be tighter

These are polish-level issues. They don't indicate systemic problems.

---

## Diagnostic Protocol

When evaluating a piece:

1. Scan for Critical Failures first. If any are present, flag immediately.
2. Scan for Severe Failures. Flag any found.
3. Count Moderate Failures. Sum the penalties.
4. Note Minor Failures for polish recommendations.
5. Calculate total score: Start at 100, subtract failure penalties, apply caps.
