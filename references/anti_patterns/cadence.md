# Cadence Signatures

Cadence is the rhythmic fingerprint of a writer. AI has identifiable cadence
patterns. Detecting and breaking them is one of the highest-leverage moves for
producing genuinely human-sounding output.

---

## LinkedIn Cadence

Signature:
- Short declarative sentence.
- Line break.
- Another short declarative.
- Line break.
- "And that changes everything."

Trigger: 3+ consecutive sentences under 10 words separated by line breaks, ending
with a dramatic one-liner.

Fix: Combine into flowing prose. Vary sentence lengths. Remove the manufactured punchline.

---

## Startup Guru Cadence

Signature:
- "Most people think X."
- "They're wrong."
- "Here's why:"
- [List of 3-5 items]

Trigger: Contrarian opener + "they're wrong" + enumerated support.

Fix: State the actual insight directly. If the contrarian frame is earned, build
the argument before revealing the reversal — don't announce it.

---

## TED Talk Cadence

Signature:
- "What if I told you..."
- Provocative claim
- Personal anecdote (usually 2-3 sentences)
- "And that's when I realized..."
- Universal lesson extracted from personal moment

Trigger: Hypothetical opener + anecdote + lesson extraction pattern.

Fix: Drop the hypothetical framing. Start with the observation. If the anecdote
matters, let it carry weight on its own without the "what if" crutch.

---

## Fake Profundity Cadence

Signature:
- [Abstract noun] is [another abstract noun].
- "Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication."
- "Connection is the currency of the future."
- "Vulnerability is strength."

Trigger: [Abstract] + copula + [Abstract], presented as revelation.

Fix: If the insight is real, prove it with specifics. If it's a platitude, delete it.
No aphorism earns its place without the argument that precedes it.

## LLM List Rhythm

Signature:
- Sentence between 15-20 words.
- Another sentence between 15-20 words.
- A third sentence between 15-20 words.
- Same syntactic structure (Subject-Verb-Object) across all three.
- Uniform comma placement.

Trigger: 3+ sentences with word count within ±4 of each other AND matching
syntactic structure.

This is THE statistical signature that AI detectors catch. Breaking it requires
deliberate variance injection.

Fix:
- After any sentence of 15-20 words, the next must be ≤10 or ≥25.
- Vary syntactic structure: inversion, fragment, compound, complex, question.
- Insert a short punch sentence. Then a long winding one with subordinate clauses
  and a parenthetical observation embedded in the middle, the kind of sentence a
  human writer produces when they're thinking through an idea and not editing yet.
  Then four words. See?

---

## Performative Empathy Cadence

Signature:
- "I see you."
- "I hear you."
- "This matters."
- "You're not alone."
- [Generic reassurance without specific referent]

Trigger: 2+ emotional validation statements without naming what's being validated.

Fix: Name the specific thing. "Losing that contract after six months of work is
brutal" beats "I see you" every time.

---

## Fake Decisiveness Cadence

Signature:
- "The answer is simple:"
- [Something not simple]
- or: "Here's what you need to do:"
- [List of obvious things]

Trigger: Certainty framing before a point that doesn't warrant certainty.

Fix: If the answer genuinely is simple, the simplicity will be self-evident.
Announcing simplicity before complexity is a tell.

---

## Trailer Voice Cadence

Signature:
- "In a world where..."
- Short punchy fragment.
- Another fragment.
- "One [person/team/company] dared to..."
- Dramatic pause equivalent (ellipsis, em dash, line break).

Trigger: Scene-setting abstraction + fragments + stakes language without specifics.

Fix: Name the world. Name the person. Name what they did. Specifics replace drama.

---

## The Summary Paragraph

Signature:
- Final paragraph that restates every point made in the preceding text.
- "In summary..." / "In conclusion..." / "Ultimately..."
- Adds no new insight, observation, or call to action.

Trigger: Closing paragraph with >60% semantic overlap with earlier paragraphs.

Fix: The closing should do ONE of:
- Extend the argument into implication ("if this is true, then...")
- Issue a challenge or call to action
- Land on a specific image, moment, or line that crystallizes everything
- Open a new question that the essay earned the right to ask

Never summarize. The reader just read the piece.

---

## Detection Threshold

Flag any passage that matches 3+ characteristics of any single cadence
signature above. One match is noise. Two is suspicious. Three is a pattern
that will trigger both human editors and AI detectors.
